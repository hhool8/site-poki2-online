What Is Stickman Hook?

Stickman Hook is a physics-driven platformer built around a simple but thrilling grappling-hook mechanic. With one click (or tap) players fire a hook that latches onto nearby anchors, creating a pendulum swing. The goal is to chain swings, manage momentum, and release precisely to land on the next anchor or platform. Packed with creative level design and satisfying physics, Stickman Hook is instantly accessible yet highly replayable.

How to Play

Fire the grappling hook at an anchor and hold to swing. Release at the correct moment to launch forward with momentum toward the next target. Later levels mix moving anchors, rotating obstacles, and shrinking platforms that require precise timing and planning. The game rewards rhythm and a feel for momentum rather than raw twitch reaction.

Controls

  • Click / Tap & Hold — fire and maintain the hook
  • Release — let go and fly forward
  • Swipe (mobile) — aim the hook or trigger precise releases

Beginner Tips

Time your release. The ideal release point is near the swing's apex, which translates momentum into distance. Practice in easier levels to internalize the rhythm.

Plan two swings ahead. Look for the next anchor while swinging toward the current one; anticipating your path prevents last-second mistakes.

Use slow runs to learn. When encountering a new world or obstacle, play slowly the first time to map anchor positions and moving patterns.

Advanced Techniques

  • Momentum chaining — sometimes skipping an intermediate peg preserves speed and lands you closer to a distant anchor for larger leaps.
  • Edge trimming — release slightly earlier than you think on tight corridors to avoid hitting walls.
  • Controlled falls — in puzzles requiring a fall, use short taps to maintain lateral control while dropping.

Levels, Themes & Rewards

Levels are organized into themed worlds (Forest, City, Space, Seasonal) with distinct visual designs and novel mechanics like rotating peg clusters or timed barriers. Completing levels often unlocks cosmetic characters and additional worlds, giving players a steady sense of progression.
